Lesson: Common Phrases

Learn common Baghdad Arabic phrases:

  • Thank you: Shukran (pronounced: shoo-kran)
  • Please: Minfadlak (pronounced: meen-fad-lak)
  • Excuse me: Afwan (pronounced: af-wan)
  • Sorry: Asif (pronounced: ah-seef)
  • Yes: Aiwa (pronounced: ay-wah)
  • No: La (pronounced: lah)
  • How much is this?: Besh hal? (pronounced: besh-hal)
  • I don’t understand: Ma fahimt (pronounced: mah fa-heemt)
  • Can you repeat that?: Tislam tkrar? (pronounced: tee-slam te-krar)
  • I’m hungry: Ana ju’aan (pronounced: ah-nah joo-an)
  • I’m thirsty: Ana athshan (pronounced: ah-nah ath-shan)
  • Where is the bathroom?: Wayn il-hammam? (pronounced: wayn il-ham-mam)
  • What is this?: Shino hada? (pronounced: shee-no ha-dah)
  • I need help: Ana biddi musa’ada (pronounced: ah-nah bee-dee moo-sa-ah-dah)
  • It’s okay: Tamam (pronounced: tah-mam)
  • Very good: Kteer mnih (pronounced: kteer mee-nih)
  • I don’t know: Ma a’rif (pronounced: mah ah-rif)
  • Let’s go: Yallah nroo7 (pronounced: yah-lah n-rooh)
  • See you later: Yallah nshufak baad (pronounced: yah-lah n-shoo-fak bah-ad)
