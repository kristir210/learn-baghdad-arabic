Practice: Greetings

Practice common Baghdad Arabic greetings with romanized pronunciation:

  • Hello: ahlan (pronounced: AHL-han)
  • Good morning: sabah (pronounced: SAH-bah)
  • Good evening: masa (pronounced: MAH-sah)
  • How are you?: shlonek (pronounced: sh-loh-NEK)
  • Welcome: ahlan wa sahlan (pronounced: AHL-han wah SAH-lan)
  • Good night: tisbah ala khair (pronounced: tis-BAH a-lah KHAIR)
  • See you later: yalla nshufak baad (pronounced: YAH-lah n-shoo-FAK bah-AD)
  • Goodbye: maa al-salama (pronounced: mah al-sah-LAH-mah)
  • What’s up?: shlonek (pronounced: sh-loh-NEK)
  • Thank you: shukran (pronounced: SHOOK-ran)
  • Please: minfadlak (pronounced: min-FAD-lak)
  • Excuse me: afuwan (pronounced: ah-FOO-wan)
  • What is your name?: shismek (pronounced: shis-MEK)
  • My name is...: ismi (pronounced: IS-mee)
  • Nice to meet you: mtsharfin (pronounced: mut-shar-TEEN)
  • How’s it going?: keef halak (pronounced: keef HAH-lak)
  • I’m fine: ana bikhair (pronounced: AH-nah bee-KHAIR)
  • Take care: khod care (pronounced: KHOD kare)
  • Have a good day: yom saeed (pronounced: yom sah-EED)
  • Congratulations: mabrook (pronounced: mah-BROOK)
