Practice: Numbers

Practice Baghdad Arabic numbers with romanized pronunciation:

  • One: wahid (pronounced: WAH-hid)
  • Two: ithnayn (pronounced: ith-NAYN)
  • Three: thalatha (pronounced: tha-LA-tha)
  • Four: arbaa (pronounced: AR-baa)
  • Five: khamsa (pronounced: KHAM-sa)
  • Six: sitta (pronounced: SIT-ta)
  • Seven: sabaa (pronounced: sa-BAA)
  • Eight: thamaaniya (pronounced: tha-MAA-nee-ya)
  • Nine: tisaa (pronounced: tee-SAA)
  • Ten: ashara (pronounced: ah-SHA-ra)
  • Eleven: ahadaashar (pronounced: a-HA-dah-shar)
  • Twelve: ithnaashar (pronounced: ith-NAH-shar)
  • Thirteen: thalathataashar (pronounced: tha-la-tha-TAH-shar)
  • Fourteen: arbaataashar (pronounced: ar-baa-TAH-shar)
  • Fifteen: khamasataashar (pronounced: kha-ma-sa-TAH-shar)
  • Sixteen: sittataashar (pronounced: sit-ta-TAH-shar)
  • Seventeen: sabaataashar (pronounced: sa-baa-TAH-shar)
  • Eighteen: thamaaniyataashar (pronounced: tha-maa-nee-YAH-shar)
  • Nineteen: tisaataashar (pronounced: tee-saa-TAH-shar)
  • Twenty: ishrun (pronounced: ISH-run)
