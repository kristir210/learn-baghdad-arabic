Practice: Greetings

Practice common Baghdad Arabic greetings with romanized pronunciation:

  • Hello: Marhaba (pronounced: mar-HAH-bah)
  • Good morning: Sabah al-noor (pronounced: sah-BAH al-NOOR)
  • Good evening: Masa al-jamal (pronounced: mah-SAH al-JAH-mal)
  • How are you?: Keefak? (pronounced: kee-FAK)
  • Welcome: Ahlan bik (pronounced: AHL-han beek)
  • Good night: Tisbah bikhair (pronounced: tis-BAH bee-KHAIR)
  • See you later: Nshufak baadan (pronounced: n-shoo-FAK bah-AHD)
  • Goodbye: Fi aman Allah (pronounced: fee ah-MAN all-LAH)
  • What’s up?: Shlon halak? (pronounced: sh-lon ha-LAK)
  • Pleased to meet you: Mutasharraf (pronounced: moo-ta-shar-RAF)
  • How’s your day?: Kayf yomak? (pronounced: kah-yif yo-MAK)
  • I’m doing well: Ana bikhair, shukran (pronounced: AH-nah bee-KHAIR, SHOOK-ran)
  • Take care of yourself: Khod care nafsak (pronounced: KHOD care naf-SAK)
  • Have a safe journey: Safar sa’eed (pronounced: sah-FAR sah-EED)
  • May God bless you: Allah ybarik fik (pronounced: all-LAH yah-BAR-rik feek)
  • Peace be upon you: Assalamu alaykum (pronounced: ah-SAL-amoo ah-LAY-koom)
  • And upon you peace: Wa alaykum assalam (pronounced: wah ah-LAY-koom ah-SAL-am)
  • Long time no see: Min zamman ma shufnak (pronounced: meen ZAM-man mah shoo-FAK)
  • How have you been?: Kayf kunak? (pronounced: kah-yif KOO-nak)
  • Wishing you all the best: Kol el tawaafuk (pronounced: kohl el tah-WAH-fook)
