Practice: Common Phrases

Practice common Baghdad Arabic phrases with Romanized pronunciation:

  • Hello: ahlan (pronounced: AHL-han)
  • Good morning: sabah al-khair (pronounced: SAH-bah al-KHAIR)
  • Good evening: masa al-khair (pronounced: MAH-sah al-KHAIR)
  • Welcome: ahlan wa sahlan (pronounced: AHL-han wah SAH-lan)
  • See you later: yalla nshufak baad (pronounced: YAH-lah n-shoo-FAK bah-AD)
  • Goodbye: maa al-salama (pronounced: mah al-SAH-lah-mah)
  • What's up?: shlonik (pronounced: SHLOH-neek)
  • Thank you: shukran (pronounced: SHOOK-ran)
  • Please: minfadlak (pronounced: min-FAD-lak)
  • Excuse me: afwan (pronounced: AH-foo-wan)
  • What is your name?: shismek (pronounced: SHIH-meck)
  • Nice to meet you: mtasharfin (pronounced: mut-shahr-FIN)
  • How's it going?: keef halak (pronounced: KEEF ha-LAAK)
  • I'm fine: ana bikhair (pronounced: AH-nah bee-KHYAR)
  • Take care: khod care (pronounced: KHOD kare)
  • Have a good day: yom saeed (pronounced: YOM say-EED)
  • Congratulations: mabrook (pronounced: mah-BROOK)
